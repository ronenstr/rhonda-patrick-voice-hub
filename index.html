Day 1 · April 8, 2026

Creatine × Muscle × Brain

5 scripts

1 — Gym-bro supplement → brain supplement (~65s)

So, for the longest time I thought of creatine as one of those gym-bro things, right? Like, I don’t need to be swole. And then the effects on the brain started to really get my interest. Your body makes creatine — liver makes 1–3 g/day — but the muscle is the greediest organ. Creatine stored as phosphocreatine helps regenerate ATP faster. More reps, more volume, faster recovery. But what I didn’t expect was what it did to my brain. I’m increasingly convinced creatine is one of the most important supplements most people aren’t taking.

2 — Why I went from 5 g to 10 g (~70s)

So, there’s this study out of Germany. At 5 g/day your muscle takes it all. But at 10 g, creatine spills over into the brain. Neuroimaging showed 10 g doubled brain creatine — gray matter, white matter, thalamus. That’s why I typically take around 10 grams daily. Game changer. No afternoon slump. Could be placebo? Sure, I’ll throw that out there — but the imaging data are very real.

3 — Creatine under stress (~60s)

So, who among us is perfectly rested and un-stressed? Nobody, right? That’s where creatine shines. Dr. Darren Candow at the University of Regina: sleep-deprived for 21 hours, 20–30 g creatine — performed better than well-rested controls. Insane. My baseline is 10 g. Jet-lagged or long podcast? I go to 20–25 g.

4 — Muscle and brain: same story (~55s)

So, creatine is one of the only supplements where muscle and brain are the same conversation. Same phosphocreatine system, same ATP buffering. In muscle: more reps, more lean mass. In brain: sharper thinking, better mood, resilience to stress. Your brain is 2% of body weight, uses 20% of energy. That’s why I call it one of the two supplements nearly everyone should be taking.

5 — Finding Frelo (~75s) 🍫

So, people ask: how do you get 10 g of creatine every day? Powder is fine but it’s not a treat, right? Consistency is the whole game. Recently I found Frelo — chocolate creatine balls, 5 g of Creapure creatine monohydrate each. Gold-standard form. Actually delicious. Two gets me to 10 g. No powder, no mixing. The hard part isn’t week one, it’s day 300. Frelo solved that. I’m a fan.
